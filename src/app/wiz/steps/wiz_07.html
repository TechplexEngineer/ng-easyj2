Subsystems

  1. Subsystem configuration

    You may create as many subsystems as makes sense. A subsystem should be a distinct part of the robot that performs a particular subset of actions. To begin thinking about what the subsystems are on your robot keep these in mind:

    • The robot is the system.
    • A subsystem cannot be nested in another subsystem.

    A subsystems generally contain one or more of the following:

    • Sensors: Devices including buttons, switches, potentiometers, encoders, etc.
    • Actuators: Devices that create movement, ie: motors & solenoids
    • Actions: High level actions which the subsystem can be told to perform. These are generally methods on the specific subsystem class which change the state when called.
    • State: Variables which hold the current status of the subsystem. Generally this includes values recorded from sensors, the position of an arm.

    Some examples of good subsystems:

    • Drivetrain: including the 2,4,6 or 8 motors
    • Arm: including a pneumatic valve and a roller system
    • Kicker: mechanism which throws the ball into the goal.
